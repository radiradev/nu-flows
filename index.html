Flows Mitigate Gaussian Error Approximations in Neutrino Cross-Section Measurements

Radi Radev, Stephen Dolan,
CERN, The European Organization for Nuclear Research
Neutrino 2024, Milan
Paper Supplementary Code arXiv

Abstract

Uncertainties in neutrino-nucleus cross-section measurements are usually evaluated by considering the spread of a measurement over an ensemble of variations of systematic parameters under the assumption they are distributed as a multivariate gaussian. However, this cannot always be expected to be a safe assumption, in particular as we enter an era of systematic-limited measurements. We showcase examples in which this assumption leads to incorrect conclusions when benchmarking neutrino interaction models and propose a solution to the issue. We propose a method of directly learning the density of throws based on normalising flows - a state-of-the-art generative modelling paradigm with tractable likelihood estimation. We test our method in a realistic cross-section measurement example, showing it achieves excellent high-dimensional density estimation, surpassing the Gaussian baseline and other machine learning methods.
